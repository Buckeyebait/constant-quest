Thorofare country

Summer Pack Trips

A real wilderness expedition, outfitted top to bottom.

Our pack trips begin at the historic WYO Wild Horse Ranch and head into the Thorofare, some of the most remote country left in the lower 48. You ride in, you live out of wall tents, and you cover ground the only way it can be covered out there, on horseback and on foot.

You do not need to bring an outfit of your own. We provide all top quality equipment, horses, mules, tack, tents, guides, wranglers, meals, and a cook. You bring yourself, your personal gear off the list we send, and a willingness to ride. We handle the rest.

  • Every trip starts and ends at the WYO Wild Horse Ranch
  • Quality stock, personally raised, trained, and managed
  • Guides, wranglers, and a camp cook on every expedition

Four ways to ride

Pick the trip that fits your crew

From a hard progressive push over the high passes to gentle family rides for kids who have never been on a horse, every trip is built around the people riding it.

Most rugged

Progressive Pack Trip

A point to point expedition over Deer Creek Pass and through the Washakie and Teton Wilderness, down the Butte Creek and Thorofare Creek valleys. Long travel days, big country, and a real shot at seeing it the way few people ever do.

High odds of grizzly and elk along the way. On a still evening or early morning, you may hear wolves.

Length
8 to 10 days
Terrain
Deer Creek Pass, 10,400 ft
Travel days
Up to 8 hrs riding and walking
Good for kids

Family Pack Trip

Gentle, well broke horses and mules for riders of every level, including children who have never ridden. Easy rides a few hours each day, with daily returns to camp or some overnight wilderness camping and fishing along the way.

A good introduction to backcountry riding for families who want the wilderness without the long haul.

Riding
A few easy hours daily
Good for
First time and young riders
Wildlife focus

Wolf and Grizzly Pack Trip

A trip built around the wildlife itself, riding deep into grizzly and wolf country. You ride near tree line in the early morning and glass the open slopes for these species when they are most likely to be moving.

Good odds of seeing a grizzly, and a chance of hearing wolves. Nothing about the country is guaranteed, but few places in the lower 48 give you a better look.

Country
Deep grizzly and wolf range
Best hours
Early morning glassing near tree line

Out there with you

Wildlife you may see

This is wild country, so nothing is promised. But there are high odds of running into grizzly and elk, and a real chance of hearing wolves working the valleys in the evening or early morning.

In the meadows

Bull moose

Moose work the green valley bottoms and willow flats through the summer months.

High odds

Brown bear

This is grizzly country. Odds of seeing a bear are good, especially glassing the open slopes at first light.

Rare and quiet

Mountain lion

Lions live here too, though they keep to themselves. A sighting is a rare piece of luck.
