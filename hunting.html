The Thorofare hunt

A real wilderness elk hunt, done on horseback.

Our elk camp sits 26 miles deep in the backcountry, on the banks of the Thorofare River, in what is considered the most remote area in the lower 48 states. There are no roads in. The way you get there is the way hunters have always gotten there, on the back of a good horse.

Everything here is 100% fair chase. We hunt on horseback and on foot, and we take a limited number of hunters each season so the country and the quality of the bulls stay exactly the way they should be.

The ride to camp is roughly 7.5 hours and 26 miles. It is a long day in the saddle, and it is also the reason the hunting is what it is. Most outfits never get this far back.

Life in camp

Wall tents, padded cots, and a wood stove going.

Camp is canvas wall tents with stoves and padded cots, set up to be comfortable and safe a long way from anywhere. You sleep warm and you sleep dry.

Hot meals come out of the dining tent each morning and evening, with a packed lunch to carry in the field. Nobody hunts hungry.

Expect to be on horseback a minimum of three to four hours per day, plus a few miles of walking. This is a hunt that asks something of you, and the country gives it back.

Licenses and tags

How a nonresident draws an elk license.

Wyoming runs the nonresident elk license through a lottery. The pieces are below. Once you are booked, we walk every hunter through the application in January, so you are not figuring it out alone.

Application window
The lottery is open January 1 to January 31. The online application takes about 10 minutes.
Special Elk License
$1,950.00 for the nonresident Special Elk License, plus a $14.00 application fee.
Preference point
$52.00 per year. You buy it July through October, and it greatly improves your draw odds.
If you do not draw
You are refunded the $1,950.00. You are out the application fee, not the license cost.
We help you apply
In January we contact every booked hunter to help complete the application start to finish.

Wyoming law prohibits nonresidents from hunting wilderness areas without a guide. Our elk area is deep wilderness, and we do not offer drop camps. A guided hunt is the only legal way in for a nonresident, which is exactly what we do.

What is included, good to know

The straight details.

No surprises and no fine print buried somewhere. Here is what to plan around before you commit to a hunt with us.

  • A deposit of $1,500 per hunter reserves your spot
  • Most hunters see elk every day, between resident herds and the Yellowstone migration on the later hunts
  • Weather swings hard, from freezing to the 70s, with snow possible on any hunt. We send a gear list
  • Two guides per hunter on sheep, two hunters per guide on elk

Your meat

Handled, or take it yourself.

Your animal goes into the walk-in cooler at the ranch overnight to start. From there you have a few honest options.

Use our onsite butcher shop for commercial vacuum sealing, specialty sausages, and shipping straight to your door. Or take it home yourself, in a chest freezer in the truck bed or frozen in a checked cooler. Either way the meat is looked after.
